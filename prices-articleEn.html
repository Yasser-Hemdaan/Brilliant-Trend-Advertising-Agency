The Magic of Numbers: How to Make Customers Buy Without Feeling the Prices

The customer wants the lowest price and competitors want to acquire and you want to profit, here the challenge is to choose a price that is profitable for you and attractive to customers and better than competitors.

Here's a comprehensive guide on pricing products in a way that attracts customers and increases profits:

  1. The importance of developing product pricing strategies.
  2. The impact of smart pricing on the success of companies.
  3. Steps to successful pricing.
  4. Marketing strategies and how to choose the right products for your products.
  5. 10 tips to make prices the secret to attracting your customers.
  6. Models for companies achieving success through effective pricing strategies.

The Importance of Setting Product Pricing Strategies:

Have you ever wondered why your sales rates are declining? Your declining sales could be due to your product pricing strategy!

You must realize that the price is not merely a number on the product; it reflects the combined values, quality, and effort put into bringing the product to the market. However, even a slight overestimation in pricing can cost potential customers.

Thus, smart pricing is a powerful factor in penetrating the market, increasing the market share of the product or service you offer, and building sustainable relationships with your target audience.

The Impact of Smart Pricing on Company Success:

Understanding Product Value: When the price is appropriate and aligns with the product's quality and market needs, customers are more likely to perceive the added value they will gain from the product.

Achieving Strong Competitiveness: The right price can be a competitive advantage for your company, whether by offering the most competitive prices or by providing a unique value that stands out from competitors.

Achieving Profitability and Growth:When products are priced appropriately, companies can increase their revenues and achieve better profit margins. Sustained profitability requires a balance between delivering maximum value to customers and covering production, marketing, and profit costs.

Building a Loyal Customer Base: When customers feel that the price matches the value they receive, they become more willing to return for repeat purchases and engage with the company. Companies can establish long-term business relationships with customers.

How to Price Products Step by Step:

There are stages in pricing products that you need to go through to achieve rapid success. However, before you begin the pricing process, you need to ask yourself several questions, such as:

  • What is the nature of the product? Is it a commodity or a service?
  • What value does the product add to the consumer?
  • What is the range of prices offered by competitors in the market?
  • What is the nature of the target audience and their needs?
  • Have you studied the market environment in which you operate?
  • What are the geographical features of the market?
  • What are the production costs, including all contributing factors?

    • Step One: Analyzing Competitor Prices.

    To surpass your competitors, you need to analyze their business methods.

    Choose at least 3 primary competitors.

    Understand how they price their products and the criteria they use in marketing and customer acquisition. Determine if they offer compelling deals or prioritize customer satisfaction and premium products.

    For example, competitors may price a product at 15 riyals, but you want to offer it for 20 riyals. In this case, you need to add unique value to your product that is not available in competitors' offerings, such as longer product shelf life or superior effectiveness compared to competitors. This way, customers will be willing to pay 20 riyals because they will receive a high-quality product.

    Step Two: Identify Characteristics of Target Customers.

    Identify demographic characteristics of customers, such as age and gender.

    Understand their economic and social levels.

    Determine if your product or service is a priority for customers or secondary.

    Determine if they seek unique capabilities regardless of price or if they prioritize a lower price.

    Tailor your marketing approach based on the target demographic and the product's relevance to them.

    All of this can be accomplished through meticulous research and surveys, whether conducted electronically or in person.

    Step Three: Determine Production Costs and Profit Margin.

    Your sales must cover the costs incurred by the company in producing and delivering the product or service. Costs include raw material prices, production costs, and shipping costs.

    Determine an appropriate profit margin for the nature of the target audience and their purchasing power, as the audience will not want to purchase something whose price exceeds its true value.

    For example,Product X price = Raw material price + Production and manufacturing costs + Shipping and distribution costs + Profit margin.

    Here is a list of marketing strategies and how to choose the right solutions for your products:

    The challenge here is how business owners can select the perfect price that is considered fair by them, appealing to customers, and also superior to competitors. Below is a comprehensive list of successful pricing strategies:

    1. Value-Based Pricing Strategy:

      This strategy is based on determining the price according to the genuine value of the product, the service, or the positive impact customers can experience upon acquiring the product.

    2. Competitive Pricing Strategy:

      This approach involves monitoring competitors' prices and setting prices that could potentially be lower, thereby gaining a competitive advantage.

    3. Price Skimming Strategy:

      This method entails initially setting a higher price for a new product and then gradually lowering it.

    4. Cost-Based Pricing Strategy:

      This approach calculates the actual production cost of the product, adding a modest profit margin. It is commonly adopted by companies that are unable to invest extensively in researching the characteristics of the target audience.

    5. Penetration Pricing Strategy:

      This strategy includes commencing with lower prices to attract buyers and subsequently increasing the prices after enticing consumers with the features.

    6. Dynamic Pricing Strategy:

      This technique involves adjusting the product's price based on the timing of the sale and the quantity of demand.

    7. Price Breaks and Offers Strategy:

      This method aims to attract customers interested in discounts, coupons, and seasonal promotions perceived as advantageous deals.

    8. Psychological Pricing Strategy:

      This strategy relies on emotional and psychological influence on the consumer, creating a sense of need to purchase the product rather than relying on rational persuasion.

      It is also possible to combine several strategies to determine the pricing of your products.

    Learn About 10 Tips to Make Prices the Key to Attracting Your Customers:

    1. Thoroughly analyze data and market research to precisely understand the market and customer needs.
    2. Establish prices that genuinely reflect the real added value of your product.
    3. Choose pricing strategies that seamlessly align with the target audience.
    4. Utilize special offer strategies for discerning clients.
    5. Invest in innovative products and marketing methods that captivate customers.
    6. Maintain unwavering commitment to quality.
    7. Display flexibility in adjusting prices to mirror supply and demand fluctuations.
    8. Cultivate partnerships with distributors and suppliers.
    9. Ask customers' opinions about prices and offers and use them to improve pricing policy.
    10. Determine the target customer categories and work on developing customized pricing strategies for each category to expand the target group.

    Examples of Companies Achieving Success Through Effective Pricing Strategies:

    1. If the company sells electronic products and devices, the effective strategies for its success are:

      pricing based on added value and the benefits it provides to customers - dynamic pricing - and a comprehensive marketing strategy on all media channels to increase awareness of its products - and a strategy of innovation and continuous development based on feedback Customer and market analysis.

    2. If the company sells technological solutions, then the effective strategy for its success is:

      the value-added strategy, where the price is determined based on the solutions that meet the needs of the target group.

    3. If the company sells digital marketing services, the effective strategies for its success are:

      the strategy of adapting to customer needs - the word-of-mouth marketing strategy - the value-added focus strategy - the innovation and development strategy.

    4. If the company sells foodstuffs, the effective strategies for its success are:

      quality and diversity strategy - promotional offers strategy Price setting strategy based on production cost - the strategy of paying attention to customer feedback .

    In conclusion, at the end of this article, you have to ask yourself, do you want to be just a number in the competitive market, or do you want the prices of your products to reflect their true value? With studies, data, and smart pricing strategies, you can build sustainable relationships based on trust and quality.
